The problem · why we're different

Drones outpace humans. Two units can’t share a picture across a jammed link. And “AI that fires” can’t be accredited.

  • The pain point nobody solves — linking two units under fire. A beachhead and a convoy each see part of the sky, but fusing their pictures across a contested, jammed tactical link is where cooperative defense breaks down. Today that hand-off is manual, slow, and dies when comms degrade.
  • We fix the link. Two units fuse into one air picture over Link-16; one unit engages what the other sees; and when the link is jammed the system degrades gracefully — each unit self-protects, then re-fuses the instant comms return.
  • Deterministic gate decides in <1 ms, a human authorizes every kill (two-person, positive-ID, fratricide), and the LLM stays OFF the kill chain — it explains, it never fires.
  • We don’t compete on a better detector. We compete on a governable, jam-resilient, cooperative architecture — the linking layer between units.
[0:30–1:15] Lead with the linking pain: “The military’s real problem isn’t detecting one drone — it’s getting two units, two convoys, to share one air picture across a jammed link and act on it together. That hand-off is manual and dies under EW. We make it automatic, and jam-resilient.” Then the contrarian close: everyone puts AI *in* the loop — we kept it out, which is the path to accreditation.

Scenario 1 · normal ingress — Manual mode

Drones inbound. A human authorizes every shot.

  • Contacts ingress from the edge — grey UNKNOWN → sensors acquire → classified HOSTILE.
  • Mode = Manual: the gate names the cheapest in-range effector but pauses on a two-person human gate — nothing fires yet.
  • The operator reads the plain-English threat call, approves, and the threat is defeated — every step written to the audit ledger.
  • Maximum control, deliberate tempo. Nothing engages without a human.

Scenario 2 · saturation — Autonomous mode

Too many, too fast — machine-speed defense inside a bounded envelope.

  • A swarm saturates the airspace. Past ~6 simultaneous threats, per-target human gating is physically impossible.
  • The operator pre-authorizes a bounded AUTO-DEFENSE envelope — two-person to arm, ABORT always live.
  • The deterministic gate engages at machine speed (~0.58 µs/decision); every action is still hash-chained into the ledger.
  • Machine tempo, human-bounded — and the LLM still never touches the trigger.

Scenario 3 · contested — DDIL / jammed

What happens when we get jammed?

  • Enemy EW blackout: the radars go blind, threats drop to ⚠ TRACK LOST — we can't see or shoot.
  • COMMS DENIED → EDGE-AUTONOMOUS: the gate keeps governing locally — no cloud, no link.
  • The instant sensing returns, the system reacquires and defeats the threats it couldn't touch.
  • Cross-unit pictures degrade gracefully → self-protect → re-fuse. Jamming delays us; it doesn't beat us.

Security & feasibility 15%

A deterministic, auditable kill chain is accreditable.

  • Tamper‑evident — hash‑chained Ed25519 signed ledger → S3 Object‑Lock WORM (immutable).
  • Separation of duties — two‑person authorization · positive‑ID · fratricide / blue‑on‑blue check.
  • GovCloud IL5, KMS encryption, IAM least‑privilege · LLM off the kill chain by design.
  • Feasibility superpower: a black box that decides to fire is nearly impossible to accredit — a deterministic gate you can audit line‑by‑line is fieldable.
  • Scales & cost‑bounded: gate is <1 ms; the LLM is async and throttled to narrate a sample — so cost doesn’t grow with threat volume.

Cost & optimization 10%

The money is in sensors & effectors — we're the cheap keystone.

  • Per-site capex — Light (mobile) ~$150–450k · Standard (fixed) ~$0.5–1.5M · Heavy (base) ~$1.5–5M+. Radar / RF / EO-IR + effector dominate; our software is single-digit % of that.
  • Cloud is tiny and FLAT — Bedrock is off the hot path & throttled (~$15–75/mo/site) and doesn't grow with drone count; the deterministic gate is near-free (0.58 µs/decision, measured). Cloud ≈ 1–5% of TCO.
  • Deploy into what exists — AWS Modular Data Center (ruggedized, DDIL-built) under the $9B JWCC vehicle. No infrastructure to build.
  • Capital-light — we sell software + integration per site; the customer/partner owns the hardware. At 100 sites our layer is low single-digit % of TCO.
